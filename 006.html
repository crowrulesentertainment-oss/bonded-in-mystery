CASE 006 • DISAPPEARANCE • 1975

JIMMY HOFFA

July 30th, 1975. Former Teamsters leader Jimmy Hoffa vanished from the parking lot of the Machus Red Fox restaurant in suburban Detroit. No confirmed sightings. No recovered body. Decades of FBI investigations, organized crime theories, witness testimonies, and buried secrets transformed the case into one of America's most infamous disappearances.

Investigation Timeline

Key moments surrounding the disappearance of Jimmy Hoffa and the decades-long investigation.

Restaurant Meeting Arranged

Hoffa reportedly planned to meet with reputed mafia figures at the Machus Red Fox restaurant in Bloomfield Township, Michigan.

Final Phone Calls

Hoffa contacts his wife claiming the meeting party never arrived. Witnesses later report seeing him near the parking lot shortly afterward.

Vehicle Found

Hoffa's green Pontiac Grand Ville was discovered abandoned in the restaurant parking lot. There were no visible signs of struggle.

Federal Investigation

The FBI launches one of the largest missing person investigations in U.S. history, pursuing organized crime connections across multiple states.

Primary Theories

Competing explanations continue to fuel debate decades after Hoffa vanished.

Mob Execution

The dominant theory suggests Hoffa was murdered by organized crime figures who feared he intended to regain power within the Teamsters union.

Secret Burial Sites

Multiple claims placed Hoffa beneath stadiums, construction sites, and remote properties, though repeated excavations produced no conclusive evidence.

Witness Elimination

Investigators suspected Hoffa was lured into a controlled environment where witnesses and physical evidence could be minimized or destroyed quickly.

FBI Informant Claims

Former mob associates and informants offered conflicting confessions over the decades, creating a maze of contradictory leads and unresolved testimony.
