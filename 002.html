MH370 DISAPPEARANCE

From a dimly lit home studio, Jonathan and Jennifer Riehle reconstruct the final hours of Malaysia Airlines Flight MH370 — comparing radar fragments, satellite pings, military tracking gaps, and the final ATC transmission.

What begins as a technical breakdown quickly evolves into a deeper question: was the aircraft slowly diverted into isolation… or did something happen in a single catastrophic moment?

FINAL TIMELINE

Investigators continue debating what truly happened during the final known movements of Flight MH370. These are the critical moments reconstructed from available records and satellite data.

1:19 AM — Final Radio Contact

“Good night Malaysian Three Seven Zero.” The final acknowledged radio transmission before the aircraft vanished from civilian radar coverage.

Radar Deviation Detected

Military radar reportedly tracked an unidentified aircraft changing course westward across the Malay Peninsula after contact was lost.

Satellite Handshake Signals

Satellite systems continued receiving automated pings from the aircraft for hours after disappearance — suggesting the plane remained airborne.

Search Zone Expansion

International search teams shifted operations toward the southern Indian Ocean after analysis of satellite communication arcs.

INVESTIGATIVE THEORIES

The disappearance of MH370 generated countless theories — from mechanical failure to deliberate diversion. The case remains one of aviation’s greatest unsolved mysteries.

Pilot Diversion

Some investigators believe the aircraft may have been intentionally redirected away from its original route.

Catastrophic Failure

Others argue a sudden onboard emergency may have disabled communications while leaving the aircraft airborne temporarily.

Electrical Fire

A fire within the electronics bay has been theorized as a cause for progressive systems failure and loss of control.

Unknown Possibilities

Some aspects of the flight path and timing remain difficult to explain, leaving room for unresolved speculation.
