CASE 007 • GHOST SHIP • 1872

MARY CELESTE

December 1872. The merchant brigantine Mary Celeste was discovered drifting alone in the Atlantic Ocean between the Azores and Portugal. The cargo remained largely untouched. Personal belongings were left behind. The captain, crew, and passengers had vanished without explanation, leaving behind one of the greatest maritime mysteries in history.

Voyage Timeline

The final known movements of the Mary Celeste before the vessel was found abandoned.

Departure from New York

The Mary Celeste departed carrying industrial alcohol bound for Genoa, Italy, under the command of Captain Benjamin Briggs.

Last Logbook Entry

The final recorded entry placed the ship near the Azores several days before it was ultimately discovered drifting at sea.

Abandoned Vessel Found

The Canadian brig Dei Gratia encountered the Mary Celeste unmanned but still seaworthy, with sails partially raised and cargo mostly intact.

Crew Vanishes Without Trace

No confirmed evidence of piracy, violence, or struggle was discovered onboard, deepening the mystery surrounding the missing crew.

Leading Theories

Historians and investigators continue debating what forced the crew to abandon ship.

Alcohol Vapor Explosion Fear

One theory suggests leaking alcohol cargo created dangerous fumes, prompting the crew to temporarily evacuate the ship before disaster struck.

Severe Weather Event

Sudden storms or rogue waves may have damaged navigation equipment and convinced the captain the vessel was sinking.

Piracy or Mutiny

Though little evidence supports it, some theories claim violence or mutiny forced the crew to abandon the ship and disappear.

Paranormal Speculation

Over the decades, the Mary Celeste became linked to legends involving sea monsters, supernatural events, and unexplained maritime phenomena.
